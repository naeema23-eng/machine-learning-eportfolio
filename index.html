Welcome

This e-portfolio presents my learning and development throughout the Machine Learning module. It brings together discussion work, practical activities, seminar-linked tasks, project contributions, and critical reflections to demonstrate both technical progress and professional growth.

Purpose of This Portfolio

The purpose of this portfolio is to provide a structured and evidence-based account of my work across the module. It shows how my understanding developed from foundational machine learning concepts to more advanced topics such as neural networks, deep learning, model evaluation, and real-world machine learning risk.

Portfolio Structure

  • Discussions: collaborative discussion posts, peer responses, and summary reflections
  • Activities: practical unit work, coding tasks, analysis, and e-portfolio exercises
  • Seminars: seminar-linked preparation, outputs, and learning points
  • Projects: major assessed project work from Unit 6 and Unit 11
  • Reflections: critical reflective development across the module, including the final reflection
  • References: full Harvard-style reference list
  • About: background, context, and professional interest in machine learning

Module Development Themes

Across this module, I developed knowledge in exploratory data analysis, regression, clustering, artificial neural networks, optimisation, deep learning, model evaluation, and the wider ethical, social, and professional issues surrounding machine learning systems. A particular theme throughout this portfolio is the importance of designing machine learning systems that are not only accurate, but also reliable, interpretable, and suitable for real-world use.
