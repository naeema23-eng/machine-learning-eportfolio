Overview

This reflection critically evaluates my learning journey throughout the Machine Learning module. It focuses not only on technical skills, but also on how my understanding evolved from basic implementation to a more analytical, critical, and context-aware perspective.

From Implementation to Understanding

At the beginning of the module, my focus was primarily on understanding how machine learning models work at a technical level. Activities such as regression, clustering, and early neural network exercises helped build a strong foundation in data preprocessing, model training, and evaluation. However, my initial approach was largely implementation-focused, where success was measured by whether a model worked rather than how well it was understood.

As the module progressed, particularly through Units 6 and 11, my perspective shifted significantly. I began to understand that building a model is only one part of the process. Interpreting results, identifying limitations, and connecting outputs to real-world contexts became equally important.

“A working model is not necessarily a useful model — its value depends on context, data quality, and interpretability.”

Development Through Practical Work

The Unit 6 team project was a key turning point in my learning. Working with the Airbnb dataset showed me how machine learning is applied in real-world scenarios where data is noisy, incomplete, and influenced by multiple factors. It became clear that preprocessing and feature engineering are often more important than the model itself.

Leading the technical aspects of the project also strengthened my ability to structure workflows, ensure consistency, and integrate different components into a coherent analysis. I learned that collaboration in data science is not just about dividing tasks, but about maintaining alignment and quality across the entire pipeline.

In Unit 11, working independently on deep learning models provided a different kind of challenge. Designing and evaluating a CNN and comparing it with MobileNetV2 required a deeper level of understanding. This project highlighted that higher model complexity does not guarantee better performance, especially when constrained by dataset limitations such as image resolution and class similarity.

Critical Thinking and Model Limitations

One of the most important developments in my learning was recognising the limitations of machine learning models. Earlier in the module, I viewed performance metrics such as accuracy and R² as indicators of success. However, through practical work and evaluation tasks, I learned that these metrics must be interpreted carefully.

For example, the CNN model achieved around 80% accuracy, but confusion matrix analysis showed consistent misclassification between similar classes. This demonstrated that model performance is not only about numerical results, but also about understanding where and why errors occur.

This shift toward critical evaluation also applied to classical models, where I observed that linear regression often failed to capture complex relationships. These experiences reinforced the importance of selecting appropriate models and understanding their assumptions.

Ethical and Real-World Awareness

The discussion activities significantly expanded my understanding of machine learning beyond technical performance. Topics such as AI reliability, bias, hallucination, and system failure highlighted the risks associated with deploying machine learning in real-world environments.

In particular, analysing AI writers and system failures in healthcare demonstrated that machine learning systems must be designed with human oversight, transparency, and accountability. This introduced a more socio-technical perspective, where systems are evaluated not only by efficiency but also by safety and trustworthiness.

This awareness is especially important in my professional context, where machine learning may be applied in healthcare and emergency systems. In such environments, incorrect outputs can have direct real-world consequences, making responsible design essential.

Overall Reflection

Overall, this module has transformed my understanding of machine learning from a purely technical discipline into a broader analytical and professional field. I have developed not only the ability to build models, but also to evaluate them critically, understand their limitations, and consider their real-world impact.

A key theme throughout my learning is that effective machine learning systems must balance performance with reliability, interpretability, and ethical responsibility. This perspective will be essential as I continue to develop my skills and apply machine learning in professional contexts.

“Machine learning is not just about prediction — it is about making reliable, responsible, and meaningful decisions from data.”
