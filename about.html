About Me

My name is Naeema Alnaqbi, and I am currently pursuing a Master’s degree in Artificial Intelligence at the University of Essex Online. My academic journey builds on my background in biomedical engineering, where I developed a strong interest in applying technology to real-world healthcare challenges.

I currently work in the healthcare and emergency services environment, where I am involved in medical equipment management, operational systems, and regulatory processes. This experience has shaped my perspective on how technology, data, and systems interact in high-risk, real-world settings.

Profile Summary

This portfolio reflects my development as a learner working at the intersection of machine learning, healthcare, and professional practice. Throughout the module, I have aimed to move beyond simply applying algorithms by developing a stronger understanding of reliability, interpretability, risk, and ethical responsibility in machine learning systems.

Key Interests

  • Machine learning in healthcare and emergency services
  • Decision-support systems in high-risk environments
  • Data quality, reliability, and robustness under uncertainty
  • Interpretable and safe AI systems
  • Neural networks, deep learning, and applied model evaluation

Professional Perspective

From a professional perspective, I aim to bridge the gap between artificial intelligence and healthcare practice. My goal is to contribute to the development of intelligent systems that are not only technically effective, but also safe, interpretable, and aligned with real-world operational requirements.

This includes an interest in areas such as early patient deterioration detection, decision-support systems, and the integration of machine learning into emergency and prehospital care environments.

About This Portfolio

This e-portfolio has been developed as part of the Machine Learning module to demonstrate my academic and professional development. It includes evidence of my work across discussions, practical activities, seminar participation, and project-based learning.

The portfolio is designed not only to present completed work, but to reflect the progression of my understanding from foundational concepts to more advanced topics such as neural networks, deep learning, and model evaluation. It also highlights my ability to critically evaluate machine learning systems within real-world contexts.
