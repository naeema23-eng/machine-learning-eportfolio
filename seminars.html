Overview

The seminar sessions in this module supported the transition from theoretical understanding to practical application. Through guided tasks, demonstrations, and preparation activities, I engaged with key machine learning concepts including regression, clustering, neural networks, and model evaluation.

Engagement and Learning Approach

Although seminar participation varied across the module, I consistently engaged with the preparatory materials, including lecture content, guided exercises, and applied tasks. This included working through datasets, reviewing modelling steps, and interpreting outputs in relation to theoretical concepts.

For example, in early units, seminar preparation involved reviewing exploratory data analysis techniques and regression modelling workflows. This reinforced key concepts such as correlation analysis, data preprocessing, and model evaluation. These activities were directly applied in my own work on the Auto MPG dataset, where I analysed missing values, skewness, and feature relationships before modelling.

In later units, seminar activities focused on clustering techniques, neural networks, and optimisation methods. Tasks such as implementing K-Means clustering, analysing the elbow method, and understanding backpropagation provided a deeper understanding of how machine learning models learn and adapt.

By Unit 11, seminar preparation extended to model evaluation and deployment concepts, including performance metrics, hyperparameter tuning, and MLOps. This helped connect technical modelling work with real-world system considerations such as scalability, monitoring, and reliability.

Key Learning Outcomes from Seminars

The seminar activities contributed to several important learning outcomes. First, they reinforced the importance of linking theory to practice. Concepts such as regression, clustering, and neural networks became more meaningful when applied to real datasets and interpreted through visualisations and model outputs.

Second, the seminars highlighted that machine learning is not only about building models, but also about understanding their limitations. Tasks involving model evaluation and comparison demonstrated that performance metrics must be interpreted carefully, particularly in complex or noisy datasets.

Finally, the seminars supported my development as an independent learner. Even when direct participation was limited, engaging with the materials and completing tasks independently allowed me to maintain consistent progress and deepen my understanding of the subject.

Reflection on Seminar Learning

Overall, the seminars played a supporting but important role in my learning journey. They provided structured opportunities to apply theoretical knowledge, clarify complex concepts, and prepare for both the practical activities and the assessed projects in the module.

This experience reinforced the importance of active engagement with learning materials, even outside formal participation. It also highlighted that meaningful learning in machine learning comes not only from attending sessions, but from applying concepts, analysing results, and reflecting on outcomes.
