Practical Activities and E-Portfolio Work

This page presents the practical activities completed across the Machine Learning module. It includes coding-based tasks, analytical exercises, notebook work, and e-portfolio activities that supported my technical development from foundational data analysis through to neural networks, model evaluation, and future machine learning applications.

Units 1–3: Foundations, EDA, and Regression Thinking

The opening stage of the module focused on foundational machine learning concepts and the importance of understanding data before modelling. My practical work in this stage included exploratory data analysis using the Auto-MPG dataset, with attention to missing values, skewness, kurtosis, correlation, and scatter plot interpretation.

These activities showed that data preparation is not a routine preprocessing step, but a critical stage that shapes the validity, reliability, and interpretability of model outcomes.

Evidence to include later: EDA write-up, correlation heatmap, scatter plot, code output, early reflection notes.

Units 4–6: Classical Machine Learning and Clustering

In this phase, I developed my understanding of supervised and unsupervised learning through regression, similarity measures, and clustering. My work included correlation and regression analysis with economic data, Jaccard coefficient calculations for asymmetric binary attributes, clustering reflections, and K-Means implementation with evaluation using the elbow method and silhouette score.

These tasks strengthened my understanding of model assumptions, scaling, interpretation, and the limitations of simple algorithms when applied to real-world datasets.

Evidence to include later: regression outputs, Jaccard calculations, clustering reflection, elbow plot, K-Means results.

Units 7–8: Artificial Neural Networks and Learning Mechanisms

In Units 7 and 8, I explored the foundations of artificial neural networks through perceptron activities, the AND operator, multilayer perceptrons, sigmoid activation, gradient descent, and backpropagation. These activities provided the conceptual bridge from classical machine learning to deeper neural architectures.

This stage was especially important because it helped me understand how neural networks learn through weight adjustment, error reduction, and non-linear activation, rather than simply memorising data.

Evidence to include later: perceptron outputs, AND activity, XOR results, sigmoid function work, gradient descent plots.

Units 9–10: CNNs, Deep Learning, and Critical Awareness

In this stage, my work moved into convolutional neural networks and wider deep learning themes. These units expanded my technical understanding while also strengthening my critical awareness of issues such as overfitting, bias, interpretability, and ethical responsibility in machine learning systems.

The practical activities during this phase helped connect neural network theory to more realistic computer vision tasks and prepared me for the individual project developed later in the module.

Evidence to include later: CNN activity outputs, training screenshots, notes on overfitting, critical deep learning reflections.

Units 11–12: Evaluation, Optimisation, and the Future of Machine Learning

The final stage of the module focused on model selection, evaluation, optimisation, and future-facing machine learning themes. I engaged with model performance measurement, evaluation metrics, and the broader question of how machine learning systems should be monitored and improved in real-world use.

This phase also extended my perspective beyond current techniques by considering future developments such as self-supervised learning, neural architecture search, and edge AI, alongside the continuing importance of governance, fairness, and responsible deployment.

Evidence to include later: model performance activity outputs, evaluation screenshots, Unit 12 future trends notes, reflection extracts.

Reflection on Activity-Based Learning

The practical activities across this module were essential in turning theoretical concepts into applied understanding. They allowed me to move from reading about machine learning methods to implementing, interpreting, and questioning them in practice.

They also highlighted that effective machine learning work depends not only on coding skill, but on data understanding, critical interpretation, model evaluation, and awareness of real-world limitations.
